<p><p>Radiofrequency ablation (RFA) for cardiology is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat certain heart conditions, such as arrhythmias. It involves the use of electrical energy to heat and destroy abnormal heart tissue causing the irregular heart rhythm. A catheter with an electrode at its tip is inserted into a blood vessel and guided to the targeted area of the heart. High-frequency electrical energy is then delivered to create a small scar or lesion, interrupting the abnormal electrical pathway responsible for the arrhythmia.</p><p>The current outlook of the radiofrequency ablation for cardiology market is positive and is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. This growth is primarily driven by the increasing prevalence of cardiac diseases, technological advancements in RFA devices, and a rise in the geriatric population worldwide.
