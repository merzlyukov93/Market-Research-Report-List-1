<p><p>The microactuator market encompasses five different types: electrostatic, electromagnetic, piezoelectric, fluid, and thermal actuators. Electrostatic actuators use electrical charges to generate motion, while electromagnetic actuators utilize magnetic fields. Piezoelectric actuators involve the conversion of electrical energy into mechanical motion using piezoelectric materials. Fluid actuators make use of fluid pressure to produce movement, while thermal actuators operate by harnessing temperature changes. These diverse types of microactuators find applications in various industries, enabling precise control and manipulation in microscopic systems for enhanced functionality and performance.</p></p>
