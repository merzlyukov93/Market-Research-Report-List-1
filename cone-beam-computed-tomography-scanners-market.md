<p><p>Cone Beam Computed Tomography (CBCT) scanners are categorized into three types based on their Field-of-View (FOV) sizes: Small FOVs, Medium FOVs, and Large FOVs. Small FOVs are used for imaging specific areas like the oral cavity. Medium FOVs cover a larger portion of the head, allowing for more comprehensive imagery of teeth, jaw, and sinuses. Large FOVs provide full facial and cranial coverage, enabling the examination of temporomandibular joints, airways, and skull fractures. These different FOV sizes provide varying levels of imaging depending on the specific diagnostic requirements.</p></p>
