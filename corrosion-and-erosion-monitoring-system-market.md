<p><p>Corrosion and erosion monitoring systems are used to detect and assess the degradation of materials in various industries. There are two types of market approaches for these systems: intrusive and non-intrusive. </p><p>Intrusive monitoring systems involve physically contacting the surface of the material being monitored through probes or sensors. This approach provides accurate and localized information about corrosion or erosion.</p><p>On the other hand, non-intrusive monitoring systems do not require direct contact with the material. They rely on techniques such as ultrasonic, electromagnetic, or visual inspections to assess the condition of the material from a distance.</p><p>Both intrusive and non-intrusive monitoring systems play a crucial role in ensuring the safety and maintenance of structures and equipment by providing early detection and preventive measures against corrosion and erosion.</p></p>
