<p><p>Radiation shielding blocks are categorized into two market types based on their density: high density and low density. High density radiation shielding blocks are composed of materials like lead or concrete, which effectively attenuate radiation by absorbing its energy. These blocks are commonly used in medical facilities, nuclear power plants, and research laboratories. On the other hand, low density radiation shielding blocks are lighter and less dense, typically made of materials like bismuth or barium. They offer a lower level of radiation protection and are often employed in less critical areas like X-ray rooms or industrial facilities.</p></p>
