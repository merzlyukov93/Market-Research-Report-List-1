<p><p>Polymerization inhibitors are substances used to prevent or slow down the process of polymerization, which is the formation of large molecules from smaller molecules. There are different types of polymerization inhibitors available in the market. Hydroquinone, quinine, aromatic nitro compounds, amines, and others are some examples of these inhibitors. Hydroquinone functions by reacting with free radicals and terminating the polymerization reaction. Quinine acts as a radical scavenger, preventing the formation of free radicals. Aromatic nitro compounds work by inhibiting the formation of peroxide radicals. Amines act as chain transfer agents, interrupting the polymerization process. Other inhibitors may include substances with similar functionalities for preventing undesired polymerization reactions.</p></p>
