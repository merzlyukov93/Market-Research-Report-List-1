<p><p>Fat mimetics are substances that mimic the texture, taste, and functionality of fats but with lower calorie content. The market for fat mimetics can be divided into three types: carbohydrate-based, protein-based, and lipid-based. Carbohydrate-based fat mimetics are derived from starches or fibers and are used to replace fats in various food products. Protein-based mimetics are made from proteins such as whey, caseins, or soy and provide creaminess and emulsion properties. Lipid-based mimetics are fatty substances like vegetable oils or emulsifiers that mimic the texture and mouthfeel of fats in food products.</p></p>
